The Best Memories

Team-1

The union of a team is the strength needed to overcome obstacles. In a given stretch of the river, a distance of approximately 2 to 3 minutes is run. The team that covers the given distance in the shortest time wins the race. It consists of the competition of two rafting rafts facing each other. They start at the same time, one on each side of the river. This competition takes place on the same course as the previous test, with the difference that there are 4 buoys in the river, 2 on the left side and 2 on the right side. Each team must choose 1 buoy from each side and pass it correctly. In this way, the team that passes the buoys correctly and arrives first at the finish line wins the leg. The winning team will continue competing in the next heats until they reach the final heat and win the discipline.

Team-2

Spending a day with the family and experiencing the adrenaline is the best experience. They run approximately 8 to 12 kilometers downriver. All the rafts start at the same time (depending on the category) and the first team to reach the finish line is the winner of the discipline. This is the test in which the technical skill of each team is demonstrated. In a given stretch, between 8 and 14 "gates" are placed in the river. These gates must be passed, some downstream, some upstream. If a team touches a gate, 5 seconds are added to their final time, and if they miss the gate, 50 seconds are added to their final time once the circuit is completed.

Adrenaline

Sit in the raft, kayak or canoe, grab the paddles and in the company of expert guides launch yourself among the wild rapids, at first sight uncontrollable rivers. Meet the mysterious gorges with daring descents by waterfalls, jumping into deep water pools and climbing through natural obstacles.

Peace

In addition to the adrenaline challenges in the wild rapids of the rivers, you can also spend more relaxing moments in them or on their banks. Relax your thoughts with fly fishing, feel the river on a paddle surf board or cross it on traditional rafts.
